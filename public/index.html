It can be confusing and scary to start your fitness journey alone. Here are some tips to help you get started.

The guidelines provided here are based on information from aworkoutroutine.com Check out that page if your interested in an in-depth look at training methods.

Rest & Pain
Volume
Examples

Rest Days

Give each muscle group at least ONE rest day. (ie: if you worked legs Monday, don't do them again until Tuesday) Some studies find it takes around 1-2 days to recover depending on the intensity of the workout and individual needs
University of New Mexico: Recovery in Training


Pain vs Progress

Muscle soreness is OK and a normal part of exercise however it is important to know the difference between soreness and pain

Soreness occurs gradually after the exercise is over while pain is experienced during or immediately following exercise

If you are not sure if its soreness or pain, end that exercise early. If the feeling occurs every time you do a particular movement, even after enough rest, find another exercise that can target the same muscle group without pain.

Volume

Volume is the combined amount of exercises, sets and reps you do in a week. You want to do less volume on smaller muscle groups, because they get worked when you are doing larger muscles.

For example the Bench Press targets your chest, but also engages your triceps.

  • Big muscle group: 60-120 total reps PER WEEK.
  • Small muscle group: 30-60 total reps PER WEEK.

Big vs Small Muscle Groups

    Major
  • Chest / Back / Quads / Hamstrings
  • 60-120 reps/week
    Minor
  • Shoulders / Biceps / Triceps / Calves / Abs
  • 30-60 reps/week

Reps Per Set

    Strength
  • 1 to 5 reps per set
    Strength
  • 6 to 12 reps per set
    Endurance
  • 13+ reps per set

Compound Exercises

Compound exercises are important because they work several muscle groups at once, leading to greater overall muscle growth.

  • Bench Press

    • Targets: Chest, Shoulders, Triceps

  • Squat

    • Targets: Legs (Hamstring, Quads, Calves) & Butt

  • Deadlift

    • Targets: Legs (More hamstrings), Glutes, Back, Abs

An easy way to make a balanced exercise, is to start with a compound exercise, and then move on to isolation movements to target specific muscles.
