General Tips

Rest Days

Give each muscle group at least ONE rest day. (ie: if you worked legs Monday, don't do them again until Tuesday) Most scientific studies find it takes around 1-2 days to recover depending on the intensity of the workout and individual needs
University of New Mexico: Recovery in Training


Pain vs Progress

Muscle soreness is OK and a normal part of exercise *however* it is important to know the difference between soreness and pain

Soreness occurs gradually after the exercise is over
&
Pain is experienced during or immediately following exercise

If you are not sure if its soreness or pain, PLEASE err on the side of caution and end that exercise early, and see if the feeling occurs next time. If the feeling occurs every time you do a particular movement even after ample rest time, see if there is another exercise that can target the same muscle group without pain, and consider visiting a physician to determine if it is caused by an underlying injury.

An injury can set your training back weeks if not longer, or lead to permanent damage, so PLEASE err on the side of caution and seek a medical professional if the condition does not go away.

For more information on pain vs soreness see the following link from The Department of Exercise Science, University of Massachusetts, Amherst.
Exercise-induced muscle pain.

Volume & Reps per Set

Volume

Volume is: The amount of exercises, sets and reps you do (usually per week)

  • Big muscle group: 60-120 total reps PER WEEK.
  • Small muscle group: 30-60 total reps PER WEEK.

This means if you target every bodypart every week you are looking at a total volume of aprox 390 to 780 total reps per week.

Big vs Small Muscle Groups

Chest / Back / Quads / Hamstrings Major 60-120 reps/week
Shoulders / Biceps / Triceps / Calves / Abs Minor 30-60 reps/week

Reps Per Set

Strength Muscle Building Endurance
1 to 5 reps per set 6 to 12 reps per set 13+ reps per set

The Big 3

There are 3 major compound exercises that can work most of your body

  • Bench Press

    • Targets: Chest, Shoulders, Triceps

  • Squat

    • Targets: Legs (Hamstring, Thigh, Calves) & Butt

  • Deadlift

    • Targets: Legs (More hamstrings), Butt, Back, Abs
